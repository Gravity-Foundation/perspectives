The Challenge

The current analytics model has a structural flaw.

You centralized analytical authority in a team that doesn't own business outcomes. The metrics that drive your company (revenue, pipeline, churn, retention) are business constructs. They belong to the functions accountable for them.

Your data team is talented. But they're defining truth for a business they observe from a distance, without the authority to settle the disagreements that inevitably arise.

  • Who presents the numbers at your leadership meeting? Not the data team. Revenue, pipeline, retention: those are owned by the people accountable for the outcomes.
  • When two teams use different churn numbers, who decides? Not the data team. They pick a definition and hope everyone complies.
  • The result: competing numbers, slow turnaround, and decisions made from spreadsheets at midnight.
bygravity.com
The New Model

Three functions. Three owners.

The work called "data team" is actually three different functions that belong in three different places.

IT

Data Platform & Infrastructure

Warehousing, pipelines, data quality, access controls. Real engineering. Centralize it.

Business

Analytical Execution

Metrics, analysis, insights. Embed it in each function. AI makes this possible at scale.

Finance

Metric Governance

When teams disagree on definitions, someone with authority decides. That's finance or ops leadership.

How AI changes the game

When two teams derive the same metric differently, AI can explain exactly how each version was calculated from the source records, surface the assumptions behind each approach, and help stakeholders resolve the difference in real time. This is what semantic layers promised but could never deliver, because they required a team to maintain them manually.
